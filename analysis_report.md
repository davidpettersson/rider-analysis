# Ontario Dressage Show Entry Analysis (2022-2025)

## Data Source & Methodology

Data was scraped from the [CompeteEasy Equest Results Portal](https://www.competeeasy.com/Equest/Results.aspx) on March 1, 2026. The portal lists dressage show results across Canada and the US. Ontario shows were identified by matching show names against known Ontario venues and organizations (Caledon, Angelstone, Dressage Niagara, Kawartha, Centreline Dressage, Wits End, Royal Agricultural Winter Fair, Dreamcrest, Glanbrook CADORA, LDA Dressage, QSLB Dressage, Quantum, Queenswood, Stevens Creek, Westar Farms, Canyon Creek, and others).

**Important notes:**
- CompeteEasy data begins in 2022. No 2021 data is available on the platform, so this analysis covers 4 years (2022-2025) rather than the requested 5.
- 2025 data includes shows through the current date and upcoming shows with results already posted.
- Entry counts represent the number of rider-entries per class (a single rider competing in multiple classes counts as multiple entries).
- "Unspecified" rider status means the class was not explicitly designated as Junior, Adult Amateur, or Open.
- Some classes from smaller regional shows could not be classified into a competition level and are counted as "Unknown" (3.3% of all entries).

## Key Findings

### Overall Trends
1. **Stable participation**: Total entries have been relatively stable, ranging from 3,979 (2022) to 4,582 (2023), with a slight dip in 2024-2025. The 2023 peak reflects expanded coverage of regional shows.
2. **Growing show count**: From 39 shows in 2022-2023 to 47 in 2024 and 52 in 2025, reflecting both new show series and expanded regional coverage.
3. **Entries per show declining slightly**: ~102 entries/show in 2022, ~117 in 2023, ~92 in 2024, ~82 in 2025. This may reflect the addition of smaller regional shows with fewer entries per event.

### By Competition Level
- **Gold** shows account for the largest share of classified entries (47.2%), followed by **Bronze** (28.1%), **Silver** (20.3%), and **CADORA** (4.3%).
- All competition levels have remained relatively stable across the 4-year period.
- Silver entries showed the most consistency year over year.

### By Rider Status
- **Adult Amateur** riders are the largest identifiable group across all levels, with 5,086 total entries (29.7% of all entries, 48.7% of classified entries).
- **Open** riders account for 3,812 entries (22.3% of all entries, 36.5% of classified entries), concentrated in Gold-level competition.
- **Junior** riders represent the smallest group at 1,545 entries (9.0% of all entries, 14.8% of classified entries).
- **Unspecified** rider status accounts for 6,670 entries (39.0%), mostly in Gold and CADORA classes and unclassified regional shows.
